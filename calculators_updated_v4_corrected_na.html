KDIGO AKI Classification (Creatinine Criteria):
• Baseline: Reference creatinine value
• AKI Stage 1: Cr 1.5-1.9× baseline OR ≥26.5 μmol/L increase within 48hr
• AKI Stage 2: Cr 2.0-2.9× baseline
• AKI Stage 3: Cr ≥3× baseline OR ≥353.6 μmol/L with acute rise
• Recovery: Improvement from peak AKI stage

Anion Gap & Delta-Delta Calculator

Delta Ratio Interpretation:
• 1-2: Pure HAGMA (high anion gap metabolic acidosis)
• <1: Concurrent NAGMA (normal anion gap metabolic acidosis) with HAGMA
• >2: Coexisting metabolic alkalosis or pre-existing elevated bicarbonate

Anion Gap Delta Delta Gradient

Delta Delta Gradient Interpretation:
• ≈ 0: Simple high anion gap metabolic acidosis
• Significantly > 0: Mixed HAGMA + metabolic alkalosis OR chronic respiratory acidosis OR non-acidotic high AG state
• Significantly < 0: Mixed HAGMA + normal AG acidosis OR multiple mixed disorders

Free Water Deficit Calculator

Formula: Free Water Deficit = TBW × (Current Na/Target Na - 1)
TBW = 0.6 × weight (men ≤65y), 0.5 × weight (women ≤65y); reduce by 0.1 for age >65y

Electrolyte-Free Water Clearance (CH₂Oe)

Formula: CH₂Oe = V × [1 - (UNa + UK)/PNa]
Clinical Applications:
• Positive: Kidneys excreting electrolyte-free water (DI, excess water loss)
• Negative: Kidneys retaining free water relative to electrolytes (SIADH)
• Useful for assessing tubular concentration/dilution function

Corrected Sodium in Hyperglycemia

Formulas:
Corrected Na (mmol/L) = Measured Na + 1.6 × [(Glucose in mg/dL − 100) / 100]
Corrected Na (mEq/L or mmol/L) = Measured Na + 1.6 × [(Glucose in mmol/L − 5.6) / 5.6]

Timed Creatinine Clearance (ICU)

Formula (per period h): CrCl (mL/min) = (UCr/SCr) × (Urine Volume / (h × 60)). Units for UCr and SCr must both be μmol/L so they cancel.

24‑Hour Urine Collection Adequacy

Converted using creatinine MW 113.12 mg/mmol
Includes: (1) creatinine mg/kg/day, (2) observed/predicted creatinine (Ix equation), (3) ACR + 24h creatinine + 24h total protein “consistency check” (albumin vs non‑albumin), (4) optional 24h sodium estimates from spot urine (Kawasaki, Tanaka, INTERSALT, Mage, Uechi).

Creatinine % Change

% change = ((Current − Baseline) / Baseline) × 100. Positive = increase; negative = decrease.

Erythropoietin (EPO) Dose Adjustment

Dosing recommendations based on current hemoglobin level:
• <100 g/L: Increase dose by 25%
• 100-119 g/L: Maintain same dose
• 120-124 g/L: Decrease dose by 25%
• 125-128 g/L: Increase dosing interval by 2 weeks
• ≥129 g/L: Stop EPO
